Vikings

Introduction

    The series is based on the legends about the Viking Ragnar Lodbrok, one of the most famous heroes of Norse culture who sacked Northumbria, France and Britain.

    The series portrays Lodbrok as a curious warrior and technologically innovative, ambitious and rebellious navigator, who makes his friend Floki build a ship, to launch himself to explore the territories to the west of Scandinavia, disobeying the tribal chief, Count Haraldson, who orders to travel to the this, as every year it happened.

    The series remains faithful to the usual lifestyles of Viking adults of the time, the years in which it is based are the beginnings of the war between the Vikings against the Islamic leaders who wanted to change their religion and later in the series about the war between Vikings and Catholic Europe, which they will defeat with the brutality and intelligence with which they are characterized.

    The series also touches on various romantic themes to give the plot more depth and not just complicate things by making them look like a bunch of warriors without humanity to the public.

    The fight and war scenes are very realistic, quite interesting and artistically done, it is something worthy and with how diverted the history channel is currently, it scares the good work it can do when they want, fortunately it is on netflix to be seen by anyone hour.

Summary
  • Season 1: The plot begins at the beginning of the Viking year and follows the adventures of Ragnar Lothbrok, a brave warrior who fulfills Nordic traditions such as devotion to the gods. The first season narrates his adventures and those of his family. It is said that he is a direct descendant of Odin.
  • Season 2: After his first explorations in England during the first season, Ragnar continues to travel the world discovering new dangers. At the same time he gains more enemies with which he faces. It is about his rise in power by becoming king of Scandinavia.
  • Season 3: Focuses on the adventures of the Viking Ragnar Lothbrok and his family. The plot focuses on the advances of the Vikings through Europe and how they managed to conquer Paris thanks to a crazy plan that turns out to be brilliant. The passage of the Vikings through Wessex is also closely followed.
  • Season 4: This season has the novelty of having double the number of episodes. The season focuses on the battles between Ragnar and Rollo in France, Bjorn's voyages in the Mediterranean, and the Viking invasion of England. In this season, the prominence of Ragnar's children grows, already at an adolescent age.
  • 5th Season: In this season the character of the obismo Heahmund (Jonathan Rhys Meyers) is introduced, which was already presented at the end of the previous season. The action continues with moments of combat, the battle of York between the two great titans, Floki's arrival in Iceland or Bjorn's trip to the Sahara desert.
  • Season 6: Season 6 focuses on King Bjorn's reign over Kattegat and Ivar's adventures in Rus. Bjorn is prepared for his next mission jeopardizes his relationship with his wife. Along the way, the men face all kinds of enemies, including ambushes. The King follows in the footsteps of his father Ragnar, who faced the same situation but was able to successfully get out of it. For his part, Ivar becomes a very violent leader whose control is based on the terror of the people around him. On his way he destroys villages, rapes women and confronts men. In addition, he has to deal with his personal and family problems.
